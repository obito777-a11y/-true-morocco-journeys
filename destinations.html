01 · Marrakesh

The Red City

Marrakesh is most people's first taste of Morocco, and it rarely disappoints — the chaos of Jemaa el-Fnaa at dusk, the calm of the Majorelle Garden, riads hidden behind unmarked doors. Best paired with a desert add-on.

See Marrakesh Tours
02 · Sahara Desert

Erg Chebbi & Erg Chigaga

Morocco's two great dune fields. Erg Chebbi near Merzouga is more accessible; Erg Chigaga further south is wilder and quieter. Both are unforgettable by 4x4, camel, or a luxury glamping stay.

See Sahara Tours
03 · Chefchaouen

The Blue Pearl

Every wall, stair and doorway in the old town is painted some shade of blue. It's a slow town — best visited for two unhurried days of wandering, tea-drinking and photographs.

See Chefchaouen Tours
04 · Fes

Morocco's Oldest Imperial City

The Fes el-Bali medina is the world's largest car-free urban area — a labyrinth of tanneries, madrasas and souks largely unchanged for centuries. Go with a guide; you will get lost otherwise, happily.

See Fes Tours
05 · Rabat

Morocco's Capital, Unhurried

Less visited than Marrakesh or Fes, and better for it — wide boulevards, the Kasbah des Oudayas, and the unfinished Hassan Tower, all without the crowds.

See Rabat Tours
06 · Casablanca

The Modern Side of Morocco

Most travellers pass through on the way somewhere else — but the Hassan II Mosque, one of the largest in the world, and the Art Deco boulevards deserve a full day.

See Casablanca Tours
07 · Agadir

Long Beaches, Easy Pace

Rebuilt after a 1960 earthquake, Agadir today is Morocco's most laid-back coastal city — wide beaches, surf schools, and an easy base for day trips south.

See Agadir Tours
08 · Essaouira

Windswept & Bohemian

A fortified fishing port with Portuguese ramparts, blue boats, and a famously strong Atlantic wind that draws windsurfers from across Europe. A popular day trip from Marrakesh.

See Essaouira Tours
09 · Atlas Mountains

Berber Villages & Mountain Air

An hour from Marrakesh, the High Atlas feels like another country — terraced villages, walnut groves, and trekking routes toward Mount Toubkal, North Africa's highest peak.

See Atlas Tours
