Merzouga, Sahara

Merzouga Stargazing Camp

1 Night 2–8 Guests 4.8 (91 reviews)

The darkest sky in Morocco

Merzouga sits at the edge of Erg Chebbi — the dunes are a 10-minute walk from the village. Far enough from any city to have genuinely dark skies, close enough to reach easily from Marrakesh or Fes.

This is the most affordable way to spend a night in the Sahara — a simple Berber bivouac rather than a luxury camp, which means nothing between you and the sky except canvas.

Highlights

  • Camel ride to the bivouac as the dunes turn orange
  • Berber dinner around the campfire
  • Guided stargazing session (Milky Way visible most nights)
  • Early morning dune sunrise walk
  • Mint tea breakfast in the dunes
  • Simple, authentic bivouac (not a luxury camp)
1Day

Merzouga → Camel Ride → Bivouac

Arrive at Merzouga by late afternoon. Camel ride into the dunes as the light turns golden. Set up at the bivouac — Berber dinner cooked over a fire, stargazing until midnight.

2Day

Sunrise → Breakfast → Return

Up before sunrise to climb the nearest dune. Breakfast served at camp. Camel or 4x4 back to Merzouga by 9am.

Included

  • 1 night in a Berber bivouac tent
  • Camel ride to/from camp
  • Dinner and breakfast
  • Stargazing guide
  • Blankets and sleeping mats

Not Included

  • Transport to Merzouga (available on request)
  • Sleeping bag (recommended to bring your own in winter)
  • Alcoholic drinks
  • Travel insurance
