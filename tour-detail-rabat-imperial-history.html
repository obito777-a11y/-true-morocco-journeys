Rabat

Rabat Imperial History

1 Day 2–12 Guests 4.6 (88 reviews)

Morocco's capital, without the crowds

Rabat is Morocco's administrative capital and its most underrated city. Wide boulevards, a UNESCO-listed medina, one of the country's best kasbahs, and monuments that rival those of Fes or Marrakesh — with a fraction of the tourists.

This guided day covers the essentials without rushing, and includes lunch in a restaurant that only locals eat at.

Highlights

  • Kasbah des Oudayas and Andalusian gardens
  • Hassan Tower and Mohamed V Mausoleum
  • Rabat medina and textile souk
  • Chellah necropolis and Roman ruins
  • Lunch at a local Rabati restaurant
  • Corniche walk along the Bou Regreg river
1Day

Kasbah + Hassan Tower + Mausoleum

Begin at the Kasbah des Oudayas — the Andalusian gardens are one of the most peaceful places in Morocco. Then the Hassan Tower and the stunning Mohamed V Mausoleum.

2Day

Medina + Chellah + Lunch

Walk through the medina to the textile souk, then out to the Chellah — a medieval necropolis built over Roman ruins. Lunch at a local Rabati restaurant before departure.

Included

  • Expert local guide
  • All entrance fees
  • Lunch
  • Transport between sites

Not Included

  • International flights or train tickets
  • Personal shopping
  • Drinks beyond lunch
  • Travel insurance
