Best Time to Visit the Sahara: A Season-by-Season Guide

June 14, 2026 6 min read By Hicham Amrani

People usually ask us one question before any other: when should we actually go? The honest answer is that the Sahara rewards different travellers at different times of year, and the "best" season depends more on what you want from the trip than on any single ideal month.

Autumn and spring: the easy answer

If you want the safest bet, late September through November and again from March through May are hard to beat. Daytime temperatures sit in a comfortable range for dune walks and camel treks, nights are cool enough to enjoy a fire without needing four blankets, and the light in early morning and late afternoon is exactly what you've seen in photos of Erg Chebbi. These months are also when most other travellers go, so camps book out further in advance.

The dunes don't change with the seasons — what changes is how comfortable you'll be experiencing them, and how much company you'll have while you do.

Winter: cold nights, clear skies

December through February brings genuinely cold desert nights — sometimes close to freezing — but also some of the clearest skies of the year. If stargazing is the main draw for you, a well-equipped winter camp with proper bedding is worth considering. Days are usually mild and pleasant for walking; it's really only after sunset that the temperature drop catches people off guard.

Summer: not impossible, just demanding

July and August can push past 40°C in the middle of the day, and we generally steer first-time desert travellers away from this window. That said, if you're set on a summer Morocco trip, an early-morning camel trek followed by a shaded midday and an evening dune walk can still work — you'll just want to plan around the heat rather than ignore it.

Our honest recommendation

For most travellers booking a first Sahara trip, we suggest the second half of October or the first half of April. You'll get warm, walkable days, cold-but-manageable nights, and dune photography light that genuinely matches what you've seen online. If you have flexibility, ask us about specific dates — we track camp availability and can usually steer you toward a quieter week even within peak season.
