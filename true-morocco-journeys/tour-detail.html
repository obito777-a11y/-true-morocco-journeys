Merzouga, Sahara

Sahara Desert 4x4 Expedition

3 Days / 2 Nights 2–12 Guests 4.8 (162 reviews)

A real crossing, not a postcard photo-op

This trip takes you from Marrakesh, over the Tizi n'Tichka pass, through the kasbahs of Ouarzazate, and out to the Erg Chebbi dunes near Merzouga — Morocco's tallest and most famous dune field. You'll drive sections of it yourself with a guide riding shotgun, then hand the wheel back for the technical dune approach into camp.

Evenings are spent around a fire with mint tea and slow-cooked tagine, mornings with coffee and a view that doesn't end. This is a small-group trip — usually four to eight travellers — so it never feels like a convoy.

Highlights

  • Tizi n'Tichka mountain pass crossing
  • Ait Benhaddou UNESCO kasbah visit
  • Camel trek into the dune camp
  • One night in a private desert camp
  • Sunrise over Erg Chebbi
  • English/French-speaking driver-guide
1Day

Marrakesh → Ait Benhaddou → Dades Valley

Early departure over the Tizi n'Tichka pass, with a stop at the mudbrick kasbahs of Ait Benhaddou. Continue through Ouarzazate and into the Dades Valley for an overnight stay at a family-run guesthouse.

2Day

Dades Valley → Todra Gorge → Merzouga

Morning walk through the dramatic Todra Gorge, then on to Merzouga by early afternoon. Trade the 4x4 for a camel and ride into camp as the dunes turn gold at sunset.

3Day

Sunrise → Merzouga → Marrakesh

Early sunrise over the dunes, breakfast at camp, then the drive back to Marrakesh with a lunch stop along the way. Drop-off at your riad by early evening.

Included

  • Private air-conditioned 4x4 & driver-guide
  • 2 nights' accommodation (guesthouse + desert camp)
  • Breakfasts & dinners as per itinerary
  • Camel trek to/from the desert camp
  • All entrance fees mentioned in the itinerary

Not Included

  • International flights
  • Lunches (stops chosen along the way)
  • Travel insurance
  • Personal expenses & gratuities
